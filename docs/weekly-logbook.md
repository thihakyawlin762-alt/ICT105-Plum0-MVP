## Lab 02: IT Opportunity Scanning

### What We Completed

1. Reviewed the three initial venture ideas from Lab 1.
2. Created the opportunity-scanning document and identified six possible IT opportunities.
3. Completed technology feasibility mapping for all opportunities.
4. Applied NUF (New, Useful, Feasible) scoring to compare ideas.
5. Selected Smart Assignment Tracker as the semester project.
6. Created GitHub Issues for upcoming customer discovery activities.
7. Updated the README to reflect the selected project direction.

### Selected Opportunity

Smart Assignment Tracker

### Why We Selected It

The Smart Assignment Tracker received the highest NUF score among our main project ideas.

* New: 4/5
* Useful: 5/5
* Feasible: 5/5
* Total Score: 14/15

We selected this opportunity because assignment deadlines are often scattered across LMS platforms, chat groups, emails, and lecturer announcements. This creates a real problem for university students. The project is useful, easy to validate with students, and feasible to prototype as a web-based application within one semester.

### What We Rejected

1. Smart Campus Lost-and-Found System

   * Rejected because the problem occurs less frequently than assignment management and may be harder to validate consistently.

2. SME Inventory Management System

   * Rejected because access to business owners for customer discovery and validation may be more difficult than accessing university students.

### What We Learned

1. How to identify entrepreneurial opportunities from real-world problems.
2. How to compare ideas using the NUF scoring method.
3. The importance of selecting a project based on feasibility and user needs rather than technology trends.

### Selected Opportunity

Smart Assignment Tracker

### Why We Selected It

The Smart Assignment Tracker received the highest NUF score during Lab 02.

* New: 4/5
* Useful: 5/5
* Feasible: 5/5
* Total: 14/15

Customer discovery interviews also confirmed that students frequently struggle with assignment management because information is scattered across LMS platforms, chat groups, emails, and lecturer announcements.

### What We Rejected

Smart Campus Lost-and-Found System

Reason:
Although useful, the problem occurs less frequently than assignment tracking. Interview evidence showed that assignment management affects students more regularly and is easier to validate and prototype within the semester.

### What We Learned

1. Assumptions must be validated with real users.
2. Students use many different methods to track assignments.
3. Interviewing users provides stronger evidence than relying on team opinions.

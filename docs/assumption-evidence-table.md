# Assumption-Evidence Table Lab-3

| No. | Team Assumption                                                       | Evidence Collected                                                                                                         | Evidence Type | Strength | Decision |
| --: | --------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------- | -------- | -------- |
|   1 | Students often miss assignment deadlines.                             | 5 out of 6 respondents reported missing or nearly missing an assignment deadline at least once.                            | Interview     | Strong   | Keep     |
|   2 | Assignment information is scattered across multiple platforms.        | Respondents mentioned LMS, chat groups, emails, and lecturer announcements as sources of assignment information.           | Interview     | Strong   | Keep     |
|   3 | Students currently rely on manual methods to track assignments.       | Most respondents use notebooks, screenshots, phone reminders, or personal notes to manage deadlines.                       | Interview     | Strong   | Keep     |
|   4 | Students want a single platform to view all assignment deadlines.     | Several respondents stated that checking multiple platforms is inconvenient and time-consuming.                            | Interview     | Moderate | Keep     |
|   5 | Missing assignment deadlines causes stress and academic problems.     | Respondents reported feeling stressed, rushing work, or losing marks due to late submissions.                              | Interview     | Moderate | Keep     |
|   6 | Automatic notifications are the most important feature students need. | Some respondents already use phone reminders and considered deadline organization more important than notifications alone. | Interview     | Moderate | Revise   |

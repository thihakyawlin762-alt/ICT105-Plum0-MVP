# Customer Discovery Summary

## Project Title | Lab-3

Smart Assignment Tracker

## Target Users Studied

University students, especially first-year and second-year students who manage assignments from multiple courses.

## Number of Responses / Interviews

10 survey responses.

## Main Repeated Problem

Students often miss or nearly miss assignment deadlines because assignment information is scattered across multiple platforms such as LMS, chat groups, emails, and lecturer announcements.

## Main Current Workarounds

* Writing deadlines in notebooks
* Taking screenshots of announcements
* Using phone notes
* Using Google Calendar
* Asking classmates for reminders
* Checking LMS manually every day

## Strongest Evidence Found

Most respondents reported that assignment information comes from multiple sources and requires manual tracking. Several respondents admitted that they had missed deadlines, submitted work late, or experienced stress because they forgot assignment due dates.

## Weak Evidence or Unclear Findings

Not all students miss deadlines regularly. Some students already use calendars or personal scheduling systems effectively. More interviews are needed to determine which reminder features are most useful.

## What We Must Revise

The project should focus on helping students organize assignment deadlines from multiple sources rather than trying to solve every academic management problem. The MVP should prioritize deadline tracking and reminders.

## Decision for Next Lab

* Continue with same problem direction

## Updated Problem Statement

University students receive assignment information from multiple sources, including LMS platforms, chat groups, emails, and lecturer announcements. Because there is no centralized place to manage these deadlines, students often rely on manual methods such as notes, screenshots, and personal reminders, which can lead to missed deadlines, late submissions, and increased stress.
